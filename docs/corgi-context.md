## What We're Building

We're building the first full-stack, AI-native insurance carrier designed specifically for technology startups. Not a broker. Not an MGA. We own the entire stack — underwriting, pricing, policy administration, claims, and reinsurance.

The tagline is "Business Insurance at the Speed of Compute." That's not marketing fluff. We can get a startup founder a full quote and bound certificate in under 10 minutes. The traditional process takes 3–6 weeks and involves 8–12 human touchpoints. We collapsed that into a form, an algorithm, and a Slack notification.

We cover everything a tech startup needs: Commercial General Liability, D&O, Tech E&O, Cyber, EPLI, Media Liability, AI Liability (which we pioneered — no one else offers this), Hired & Non-Owned Auto, Fiduciary Liability, and Reps & Warranties. Modular. Toggleable. No broker sitting in the middle taking a cut and slowing everything down.

We're not building a slightly better version of what exists. We're rebuilding insurance infrastructure from scratch, designed to last 100 years.

---

## The Problem

Startups move at a pace that the insurance industry was never designed to serve. A founder who just closed a seed round needs D&O insurance before their first board meeting. A Series A company needs updated cyber coverage before they can close an enterprise contract. Today, they call a broker, wait a week, get a PDF, sign it manually, and hope the coverage is actually right.

The industry runs on legacy systems, manual workflows, and a wholesale-broker-carrier relay that extracts margin at every step and adds weeks of friction. The people who built those systems weren't building for founders. They were building for risk-averse actuaries at large corporations.

We were those founders. We needed this product. It didn't exist. So we built it.

**What our best customers have in common:**

- Moving fast and don't want insurance to slow them down

**Who is NOT our customer:**

## Competitors and How I Think About Them

**Vouch:** The closest comp. They started as a broker/MGA, acquired StartSure, and are now pushing toward carrier status. Their product is decent but they're still built on legacy infrastructure and don't have our speed or AI-native underwriting. We respect them. We're faster and cheaper.

**Embroker:** Tech-focused but they're a broker — they don't own underwriting. They're reliant on third-party carriers, which means they can't iterate on pricing or coverage at our pace. Good UX, but fundamentally a different business model.

**Founder Shield:** Startup-focused, broker model, solid reputation. They win on relationships. We win on speed and automation.

**Koop:** Narrower coverage set, earlier stage company. Haven't seen them as a real threat at scale yet.

**Traditional carriers (Hiscox, Markel, etc.):** These are the incumbents we're replacing. Slow, expensive, relationship-dependent, built for corporate risk managers not startup founders.

**Where we win:** Speed (10 minutes vs. weeks), AI-native underwriting, modular/toggleable coverage, founder-first UX, AI Liability coverage no one else offers.

**Where we lose:** Enterprise accounts that want a human relationship manager. We're fixing this with our Client Success team.

## Business Model

We're a full-stack carrier. That matters because:

1. We collect premiums and own the risk — we're not clipping a fee off someone else's policy.
2. We earn underwriting profit AND investment income on float. Traditional brokers only earn commission.
3. We control the pricing algorithm — we can move faster and more precisely than anyone relying on a third-party carrier.

We received our carrier license in July 2025. We became a licensed captive reinsurer in late 2025. Every step up the stack increases our margin and our competitive moat.

**Revenue model:** Annual premiums, paid upfront or monthly. Average customer pays ~$1,000/year. We have 40,000+ customers. ARR is $40M+ as of December 2025 — 9 months after full carrier approval.

**Churn:** Below 1%. This is the number I'm most proud of. When founders renew, it's a vote of confidence in the product and the experience.

## Risks and Open Questions

- **Regulatory complexity at scale:** We're in 49 states. As we grow, state-level regulatory relationships become a full-time function. One regulatory dispute in a major state could slow us down significantly.
- **Reinsurance dependency:** Even as a captive, we're exposed to reinsurance market conditions. A hard market could compress our margins.
- **Underwriting model accuracy:** Our pricing AI is the moat. If loss ratios creep up because we mispriced risk at scale, that's an existential problem. We're watching this closely.
- **Competition for talent:** Hiring people who understand both insurance regulation AND modern engineering is genuinely hard. The Venn diagram is small.
- **Brand awareness outside YC network:** We're well known in YC-adjacent startup world. Breaking into other ecosystems requires deliberate GTM effort.

## Why This, Why Now, Why Us

The insurance industry is a $1 trillion+ market running on infrastructure built in the 1970s. The only reason it hasn't been disrupted is because the regulatory moat is real — you can't just build an app and call yourself an insurer. We spent years getting licensed.

AI changes the unit economics completely. Manual underwriting that took 3 weeks of human analyst time now takes seconds. Claims processing that required adjusters now uses computer vision and structured data. We're not using AI to put a chatbot on top of a legacy system. We built the whole thing natively.

The timing: cloud-native startups are the fastest-growing segment of the insurance market. They're also the most underserved. Every traditional carrier has a startup product that was designed as an afterthought. We designed it as the main event.

Why us: Nico and Emily have built and scaled a product to 200M users before. We know how to move fast without breaking things that matter. And unlike most insurtech founders, we actually understand the regulatory infrastructure from the inside out.
